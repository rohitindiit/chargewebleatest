Types of Electric Vehicles and How ChargEasy Can Help

19 Dec 2023

There are many different types of electric vehicles (EVs) available on the market today, each with its own unique advantages and disadvantages. Some of the most common types of EVs include:

Battery electric vehicles (BEVs): BEVs are the most common type of EV, and they run entirely on electricity stored in batteries. BEVs have zero tailpipe emissions and are the most environmentally friendly type of EV.

Plug-in hybrid electric vehicles (PHEVs): PHEVs combine a gasoline-powered engine with an electric motor and battery. PHEVs can run on gasoline or electricity, and they can be plugged in to recharge their batteries. PHEVs typically have a shorter range than BEVs, but they offer more flexibility.

Mild hybrid electric vehicles (MHEVs): MHEVs use a small electric motor to assist the gasoline engine. MHEVs cannot be plugged in to recharge their batteries, and they typically have a very short range.

Fuel cell electric vehicles (FCEVs): FCEVs use hydrogen to generate electricity, which powers the electric motor. FCEVs have zero tailpipe emissions and a longer range than BEVs, but they are less common and more expensive.

No matter what type of EV you have, the ChargEasy app can help you find a charging station with the right adapter. ChargEasy lists charging stations by connector type, so you can easily find a station that is compatible with your EV.

Here are some tips for using the ChargEasy app to find a charging station with the right adapter:

Open the ChargEasy app and search for charging stations near you.

Tap on a charging station to view its details.

Scroll down to the "Connector Types" section and view the list of supported connector types.

Make sure that the charging station supports the connector type for your EV.

If you are unsure of which connector type is compatible with your EV, you can consult your owner's manual.

Here are some examples of different types of EV connectors:

J1772: This is the most common connector type for BEVs and PHEVs in the United States.

CCS1: This is a popular connector type for BEVs in Europe.

CHAdeMO: This is a popular connector type for BEVs in Japan.

Tesla: Tesla vehicles use their own proprietary connector type.

The ChargEasy app is a valuable tool for EV owners, as it can help them find charging stations with the right adapters. With ChargEasy, you can be confident that you will always be able to find a place to charge your EV, no matter where you are.
