EV Charging Etiquette: Be a Good Citizen of the Electric Road

19 Dec 2023

As more and more people switch to electric vehicles (EVs), it's important to be aware of EV charging etiquette. By following a few simple guidelines, you can help to ensure that everyone has a fair chance to charge their EV and that we all keep our roads clean and green.

Here are a few tips:

Only park in EV charging spots when you're actually charging. It's rude to tie up a charger for hours if you're not using it.

Don't unplug another EV. It's important to respect other EV drivers and their need to charge.

Be mindful of how long you charge. If you're only charging to 80%, move to another spot if there are other EVs waiting.

Use the right charger for your EV. Not all chargers are compatible with all EVs. Make sure you're using the right charger to avoid damaging your vehicle or the charger itself.

Keep the charging area clean and tidy. Don't leave trash behind or block other vehicles from accessing the chargers.

Here are a few additional tips to be a good citizen of the electric road:

Plan ahead. When you're taking a road trip, use a charging station app to plan your route and identify charging stations along the way.

Be considerate of other EV drivers. If you see a line of EVs waiting at a charger, offer to let someone go ahead of you if they're in a hurry.

Educate others about EVs. If you see someone looking confused at a charging station, offer to help them out.

-Kelly A Norton
