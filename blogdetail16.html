The Top 10 EV Books every EV owner should have:

19 Dec 2023

Why are some countries consuming EVs at a faster rate than others?

There are a number of reasons why some countries are consuming EVs at a faster rate than others. Here are a few of the most important ones:

1. The Electric Vehicle Revolution: A History of the Future by Steven Johnson

This book provides a comprehensive history of electric vehicles, from their early days in the late 1800s to their present-day resurgence. Johnson explores the technological, economic, and social factors that have driven the EV revolution, and he offers a glimpse of what the future holds for electric transportation.

2. The Electric Vehicle Conversion Handbook by Mark J. Warner

This book is a practical guide to converting a gas-powered car to an electric vehicle. Warner covers everything from choosing the right components to installing them in your car. He also provides tips on troubleshooting and maintaining your converted EV.

3. DIY Lithium Batteries: How to Build Your Own Battery Packs by Micah Toll

This book is a step-by-step guide to building your own lithium battery packs. Toll covers everything from the basics of battery chemistry to the safety precautions you need to take when working with lithium batteries. He also provides instructions on building a variety of different battery packs, from small packs for electric bikes to large packs for electric cars.

4. Electric Vehicles for Dummies by Bill Holland

This book is a beginner's guide to electric vehicles. Holland covers everything from the basics of how EVs work to the different types of EVs available on the market. He also provides advice on choosing the right EV for your needs and how to get the most out of your EV.

5. Charging the Nation: How America's Electric Vehicle Revolution Will Transform Transportation and Save Our Planet by Michael Liebreich

This book explores the challenges and opportunities of the electric vehicle revolution. Liebreich discusses the need to upgrade America's charging infrastructure, the impact of EVs on the environment, and the economic benefits of EVs.

6. The Plug-in Electric Vehicle: How it Will Revolutionize the Way We Drive by Tom Gage

This book provides a comprehensive overview of plug-in electric vehicles. Gage discusses the technology behind EVs, the different types of EVs available, and the benefits of owning an EV. He also addresses some of the common concerns about EVs, such as range anxiety and charging time.

7. The Electric Vehicle Market: A Global Perspective by Navigant Research

This book provides a detailed analysis of the global electric vehicle market. Navigant Research covers everything from the sales of EVs to the growth of the EV charging infrastructure. They also provide forecasts for the future of the EV market.

8. The Electric Vehicle Ecosystem: Opportunities and Challenges by PwC

This book explores the electric vehicle ecosystem, which includes the manufacturers, suppliers, and service providers that support the EV industry. PwC identifies the key opportunities and challenges facing the EV ecosystem, and they provide recommendations for how to overcome these challenges.

9. The Electric Vehicle Infrastructure: A Global Perspective by McKinsey & Company

This book provides a comprehensive overview of the global electric vehicle infrastructure. McKinsey & Company covers everything from the different types of EV chargers to the challenges of deploying EV chargers at scale. They also provide recommendations for how to accelerate the deployment of EV chargers.

10. The Electric Vehicle Revolution and Its Impact on Energy Markets by Wood Mackenzie

This book explores the impact of the electric vehicle revolution on energy markets. Wood Mackenzie discusses the impact of EVs on electricity demand, the need for new renewable energy sources, and the impact of EVs on the oil and gas industry.

These are just a few of the many great books available on electric vehicles. Whether you're a seasoned EV owner or a first-time buyer, there's a book out there that's perfect for you.

-Kelly A Norton
